About BISV Hacks

What is BISV Hacks?

BISV Hacks is an inclusive, student-led hackathon organized by the Programming for Data Science & Machine Learning Club at BASIS Independent Silicon Valley in San Jose, CA. The hackathon will run throughout the day, hosting participants from many different areas.

Our Mission

Our event is designed to encourage creativity and collaboration by bringing together students of all experience levels. Participants will either form teams or work solo to develop innovative projects, solve real-world challenges, and gain valuable experience.

What to Expect

In addition to coding, attendees will have the opportunity to hear from inspiring speakers who are industry professionals, and engage in hands-on workshops. We also provide lunch, snacks, and refreshments to fuel your creativity throughout the day.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event in which a large number of people meet to engage in collaborative or competitive computer programming. In our case, competitive programming.

Can I submit projects from other hackathons?

As long as it pertains to the theme and prompt, absolutely!

Who can participate? Is coding experience needed?

Any high school student with a passion for technology can participate, even if they have no coding experience!

Do I need a team?

Participants can come solo or with up to 6 teammates.

Will the hackathon be judged in-person or online?

The hackathon will be judged online and not in-person due to time constraints. The results will be sent out by 5:00 p.m. on March 2nd.

Where will the hackathon be held?

The hackathon will be held at BASIS Independent Silicon Valley. The school's adress is 1290 Parkmoor Ave, San Jose, CA 95126.

What do I need to bring?

Just you and your laptop!

Is there going to be food?

Yes, lunch will be held, and there will be snacks served throughout the day.
