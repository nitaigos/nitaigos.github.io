About BISV Hacks

What is BISV Hacks?

BISV Hacks is an inclusive, student-led hackathon organized by students at BASIS Independent Silicon Valley Upper School in San Jose, CA. The hackathon will last 12 hours on March 8th, 2026, hosting participants from many different areas.

Our Mission

Our event encourages creativity and collaboration by bringing together students of all experience levels. Participants will form teams or work solo to develop projects, solve challenges, and gain experience.

What to Expect

In addition to coding, attendees will hear from industry speakers and take part in hands-on workshops. Lunch, snacks, and refreshments will be provided throughout the day.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon? +

A hackathon is an event in which a large number of people meet to engage in collaborative or competitive computer programming. In our case, collaborative programming.

Can I submit projects from other hackathons? +

As long as it fits the theme and prompt, yes. In addition, the hacking time must be within our 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM timeframe.

Who can participate? Is coding experience needed? +

Any high school student with a passion for technology can participate, even if they have no coding experience!

Do I need a team? +

You can come solo or with up to 4 teammates.

Will the hackathon be judged in-person or online? +

The hackathon will be judged in-person. We'll try to announce the results by 8:00 PM.

Where will the hackathon be held? +

The hackathon will be held at BASIS Independent Silicon Valley Upper School. The school's adress is 1290 Parkmoor Ave, San Jose, CA 95126.

What do I need to bring? +

Just you and your laptop!

Is there going to be food? +

Yes, both lunch and dinner, will be held, and there will be snacks served throughout the day.
